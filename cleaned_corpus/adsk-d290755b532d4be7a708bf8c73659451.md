# Hydrogen-powered drones propel a path to sustainable air travel

H2GO Power

H2GO Power

UK start-up [H2GO Power](https://www.h2gopower.com/) is working on a solution that aims to reduce the environmental footprint of air travel: Working together with Autodesk, the company has developed an additively manufactured reactor that can safely store and release hydrogen gas to power a drone.

A hydrogen-powered drone can fly three times longer than a traditional battery-powered one, and can also carry heavier payloads. The H2GO team used [Fusion 360](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/overview) and [3ds Max](https://www.autodesk.com/products/3ds-max/overview) to design and optimize a reactor that’s lightweight, durable, efficient, and safe. The company is working on an off-the-shelf solution that expands a drone’s commercial applications—including scanning data from construction sites and disaster areas, and even delivering medicine. The long-term goal is to partner with airplane manufacturers to integrate its hydrogen storage solution into aircraft, using the right technology and insights to streamline a path to a more sustainable world.
