# What is 3D Printing? | 3D Printing Software | Autodesk

### What is 3D printing?

3D printing, also known as [additive manufacturing](https://www.autodesk.com/solutions/additive-manufacturing) , is a process that involves creating a physical object from a 3D CAD model. 3D printing is an additive process where layers of material are built up to create a 3D part.

### What is 3D printing software?

[3D printing software](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/blog/fusion-360-additive-manufacturing-3d-printing-capabilities/) is a computer program used to prepare 3d models for 3D printing. This software is to create or modify 3D designs and convert them into a format to be used on a 3D printer.

## What is 3D printing software used for?

### Prototyping

3D printing is used to quickly create prototypes for visual aids, assembly mockups, and presentation models.

### Lightweighting parts

Fuel efficiency and emissions reductions are driving the need for lightweight parts with 3D printing in aerospace and automotive applications.

### Functionally enhancing products

3D printing removes many of the constraints imposed by traditional manufacturing processes that prevent engineers from truly designing for optimal performance.

### Medical research and treatment

Dr. Hannes Schwenke uses 3D printing and scanning to produce accurate models of patient organs to treat them more effectively.

### Plastics design

Generate 3D models, virtually test tolerances, and then push them from machining to production.

### Affordable housing

Habitat for Humanity and PERI 3D Construction built the organization’s first 3D-printed home, reimagining what affordable housing may look like.

## The benefits of 3D printing

### Design flexibility

3D printing allows for the creation of highly complex and intricate designs that are challenging or impossible to achieve with traditional manufacturing methods.

### Rapid prototyping

3D printing accelerates the product development process by enabling quick and cost-effective [prototyping](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/blog/3-types-of-industrial-design-prototyping/) . Design iterations can be rapidly produced and tested, reducing the time and cost associated with traditional prototyping methods.

### Cost-effective production

3D printing can be cost-effective for low-volume or customized production. It eliminates the need for [expensive molds](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/blog/3d-printing-vs-injection-molding-what-you-need-to-know/) , tooling, and setup costs associated with traditional manufacturing methods.

### Material waste reduction

Traditional subtractive manufacturing processes often generate significant material waste, as excess material is removed during machining. 3D printing is an additive process, where material is added layer by layer, resulting in [minimal waste generation](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/blog/three-environmental-considerations-for-the-fff-3d-printing-process/) .
