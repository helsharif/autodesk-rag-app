# SketchBook | Sketchbook Software Discontinued | Autodesk

# Sketchbook is no longer available

As of June 30, 2021, Autodesk is discontinuing SketchBook. We will no longer offer downloads for SketchBook or deliver new versions or updates.

Sketchbook is now offered by Sketchbook, Inc., details are available at [www.sketchbook.com](https://www.sketchbook.com/).

## If you are currently using SketchBook Pro as part of the Media & Entertainment Collection, the Alias AutoStudio bundle, or via a standalone enterprise license

You can continue using SketchBook Pro as part of the Media & Entertainment Collection, the Alias AutoStudio bundle and as a standalone enterprise license until the end of your contract terms. Autodesk will not offer new versions, upgrades or updates after June 30, 2021.

## If you are currently using SketchBook Pro as a student or educator

As of June 30, 2021 Autodesk has discontinued the student and education versions of SketchBook Pro and will no longer offer new versions, upgrades or updates. You can continue using your existing copy of SketchBook Pro until the end of your contract terms, but new downloads are not available. Enterprise and Education users can continue to re-download their active license from their enterprise or student account portal.

## Is SketchBook still supported?

The Autodesk product support center for SketchBook Pro has been closed, however the content is still [available](http://help.sketchbook.com/). SketchBook support has transitioned to Sketchbook, Inc. If you have questions about using Sketchbook, please visit Sketchbook’s [website](https://www.sketchbook.com/) for information and to contact support for assistance.

## I am currently using the consumer version of SketchBook. How will these changes affect me?

SketchBook has spun out of Autodesk as an independent [company](https://blogs.autodesk.com/sketchbookpro/the-future-of-sketchbook/). You can continue to use your existing version of Autodesk SketchBook, but Autodesk will no longer offer updates or reinstalls of previous versions. To learn more about the next phase of Sketchbook as an independent company, visit [www.sketchbook.com](http://www.sketchbook.com)
